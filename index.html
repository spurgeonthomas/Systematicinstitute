Problem Statement

A mid-size financial services firm was operating on a fragmented CRM ecosystem across 4 business units. Customer data existed in 7 disparate systems with no single source of truth, causing compliance risk, duplicate effort, and poor customer experience. Legacy processes had never been formally documented.

Approach & Artifacts

Conducted 3-week discovery sprint with 12 stakeholder groups. Facilitated structured workshops using CATWOE analysis and SIPOC frameworks. Produced current-state BPMN swimlane models, stakeholder impact matrix, gap analysis, BRD, FRD, and full RTM. Coordinated UAT across 3 QA cycles.

Techniques Used

Stakeholder interviews · CATWOE analysis · SIPOC mapping · BPMN 2.0 · Gap analysis · MoSCoW prioritization · RTM · UAT planning

Outcome

System went live on schedule with 40% reduction in manual data entry. Requirements traceability reduced change request volume by 30%. Documented cost avoidance of $800K annually identified through workflow optimization recommendations.

Problem Statement

Five government agencies were exchanging sensitive data through manual, email-based processes — creating security vulnerabilities, audit risks, and 4–7 day reporting delays. No formal system context or data ownership had ever been documented.

Produced system context diagrams, data flow diagrams, and entity-relationship models. Facilitated 80+ stakeholder workshops across agencies. Outcome

Platform delivered 60% improvement in inter-agency reporting speed. Security posture dramatically improved through formalized data exchange protocols. $1.2M in savings identified through elimination of manual reconciliation processes.

Problem Statement

A regional health system was replacing its legacy clinical information system with a commercial EHR affecting 14 departments and over 4,000 clinical staff. Existing workflows were undocumented and varied significantly by department.

Approach & Artifacts

Conducted departmental workflow workshops to capture as-is clinical processes. Authored data mapping specifications and interface control documents for 12 integration points. Maintained a 900-item RTM aligned to HL7 and regulatory requirements. Designed UAT scripts and managed three UAT cycles.

Outcome

Go-live achieved on schedule with zero critical defects in the first 90 days. Traceability documentation reduced post-implementation change requests by 45%. Recognized as a model for future EHR deployments across the organization.

Problem Statement

Six product teams operating under Waterfall with no consistent backlog hygiene, poorly defined acceptance criteria, and frequent mid-sprint scope changes causing quality issues and missed commitments.

Approach & Artifacts

Introduced user story mapping workshops, DoR and DoD frameworks. Delivered story-writing training across 60 team members. Implemented Given-When-Then acceptance criteria templates. Produced an Agile BA playbook adopted organization-wide.

Outcome

Delivery velocity improved 35% within two quarters. Backlog ambiguity reduced by 50% as measured by rework rate and story rejection at sprint planning. The Agile BA playbook became the organizational standard for all new team onboarding.
